It is the largest e-commerce site in the Arab world, more than 400,000 products from various categories including electronics, fashion, clinical, watches, perfumes, and others.
Today, Souq.com makes more than 23 million visitors per month and is growing rapidly as the number of online shoppers in the Arab world grows.
Souq.com is often the "Amazon Middle East" and serves as a site for sale a convenient and secure shopping experience with online payment service. Established in 2005, Souq.com has been a pioneer in online shopping in the region as an auction site and is linked to the Arab Internet portal Maktoob. When Yahoo acquired Maktoob in August 2009, was not included in the deal, and Maktoob broke away to remain part of Jabbar Internet Group. Then, in early 2011, the site turned into a market model selling at fixed prices, and the retail division was launched in late 2011.
In addition to its head office in Dubai, UAE, Souq.com caters to the needs of local markets with commercial offices in Egypt, Saudi Arabia and Kuwait; and a technical development center in Jordan and India.
The shape of our logo may have evolved over the years, but our priority has not changed.
